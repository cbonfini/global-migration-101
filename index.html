Faculty Teaching Course

Global Migration 101: teaching migration with depth and care

A comprehensive, interdisciplinary teaching course on global migration designed for university faculty. Spanning 14 modules, it integrates conceptual foundations, historical and structural context, systems and governance, the human experience of migration, narratives and ethics, and future challenges. Each module provides paired undergraduate and graduate learning outcomes, signature learning experiences, peer-reviewed scholarship, and curated video resources.

How the course works

Everything a department needs to teach migration

Designed for faculty across disciplines — sociology, political science, law, geography, theology, economics, and area studies — with components you can adopt whole or piece by piece.

◎

Paired learning outcomes

Every module pairs 3–5 undergraduate outcomes with 1–3 graduate outcomes, so one course scales across levels.

❧

Peer-reviewed reading lists

Curated, citable scholarship with DOIs for each module — vetted for teaching at the university level.

▷

Video & media library

Authoritative lectures, documentaries, and explainers selected for classroom use.

◆

Signature experiences

A concrete, active-learning assignment anchors each module — from data briefs to scenario planning.

⬢

Slides & handouts

Redesigned visual lecture decks with optional narration, plus printable faculty handouts for every module.

✦

Interactive glossary

A searchable glossary of 125+ migration concepts, linked to the modules where they appear.
