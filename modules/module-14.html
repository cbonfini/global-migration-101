14 Module overview

Examines emerging migration governance approaches, humanitarian response, community-based and practical interventions, global cooperation, and future migration challenges, culminating in a capstone proposal.

Skill emphasis: Applied + Integrative

◎ Learning outcomes

Undergraduate

  1. Identify organizations and stakeholders involved in migration governance
  2. Describe policy approaches and governance solutions
  3. Explain global cooperation in migration governance
  4. Propose basic interventions and evaluate future challenges

Graduate

  1. Design comprehensive migration solutions integrating multi-level approaches
  2. Critically assess emerging global migration governance frameworks
  3. Design or evaluate migration policy strategies

▤ Peer-reviewed readings

Show 6 readings6
  • DOI'The Makings of a Success': The Global Compact on Refugees and the Inaugural Global Refugee Forum
    Gillian D. Triggs, Patrick C. J. Wall · 2020 · International Journal of Refugee Law, 32(2), 283–313 · 10.1093/ijrl/eeaa024

    Assesses the 2018 Global Compact on Refugees and the inaugural 2019 Global Refugee Forum as the first 'States plus' multilateral effort to operationalise burden-sharing and durable solutions at scale. Authoritative overview for teaching global migration governance and the political feasibility of compact-based approaches.

    Access article →
  • DOIGlobal Partnerships in Governing Labour Migration: The Uneasy Relationship Between the ILO and IOM in the Promotion of Decent Work for Migrants
    Laura Foley, Nicola Piper · 2021 · Global Public Policy and Governance, 1(2), 159–179 · 10.1007/s43508-021-00022-x

    Examines the competitive-cooperative dynamics between the ILO and IOM in global labour migration governance, interrogating whether their partnership advances or obstructs the decent work agenda. Critical for teaching multi-actor global governance and the tensions inherent in migration management vs. protection mandates.

    Access article →
  • DOIBetween Refugee Protection and Migration Management: The Quest for Coordination Between UNHCR and IOM in the Asia-Pacific Region
    Sebastien Moretti · 2020 · The Pacific Review, 33(2), 179–207 · 10.1080/01436597.2020.1780910

    Case study of UNHCR–IOM coordination in Asia-Pacific 'mixed flow' situations, revealing mandate tensions between protection and migration management. Illustrates the operational challenges of multi-level humanitarian governance and the blurring of refugee protection and migration management.

    Access article →
  • DOIFrom Local Action to Global Influence: How Cities Shape Governance in a Polycentric World
    Colleen V. Thouez, Raphaela Schweiger · 2026 · Social Sciences, 15(5), 304 · 10.3390/socsci15050304

    Analyses how cities have become actors in global migration governance—shaping the Global Compact for Migration negotiations, supporting displaced Ukrainians, and advancing the climate mobility agenda through transnational networks like the Mayors Migration Council. Ideal for teaching multi-level and polycentric governance.

    Access article →
  • DOIDurable solutions: to what and for whom?
    Maple, Nicholas; Hovil, Lucy · 2025 · Journal of Refugee Studies, 38(1), 112–130 · 10.1093/jrs/feae090

    Argues that the three durable solutions (resettlement, local integration, repatriation) are out of reach for most refugees and proposes recalibrating them around the (re)gaining of citizenship and refugees' own ground-level solutions. Excellent for prompting graduate students to critique the durable-solutions framework central to migration governance.

    Access article →
  • DOICompatible Compacts? The 'Social Life' of Vulnerability, Migration Governance, and Protection at the Zimbabwe–South Africa Border
    Kudakwashe Vanyoro, Nicholas Maple, Jo Vearey · 2024 · African Human Mobility Review, 10(1) · 10.14426/ahmr.v10i1.1719

    Challenges the neat refugee/migrant distinction embedded in the Global Compacts at the Zimbabwe–South Africa border, proposing the concept of the 'social life of vulnerability' to capture fluid protection needs. Directly addresses the governance gaps in the compact architecture and the ethics of categorisation.

    Access article →

▷ Video resources

Show 4 videos4
YouTube The United Nations Global Compact for Migration — Aspirations and Realities
Cambridge Development & Alumni Relations / University of Cambridge · 2021 · 51:07

A University of Cambridge lecture examining the 2018 Global Compact for Safe, Orderly and Regular Migration — its aspirations, the controversy around its adoption (including U.S. withdrawal), and its implementation during the pandemic. An authoritative academic resource for understanding the GCM as both policy achievement and political battleground.

YouTube What Is the Global Compact for Migration?
United Nations · 2018 · 2:32

The UN's official two-minute explainer on the Global Compact for Safe, Orderly and Regular Migration (GCM), clarifying its purpose, non-binding nature, and key objectives. A brief orientation video to play before class discussion or before screening the Cambridge lecture.

YouTube Philosophies of Migration Governance in a Globalizing World | Prof. Antoine Pécoud | UNU-MERIT
UNU MERIT (United Nations University) · 2021 · 58:19

University of Sorbonne Paris Nord professor Antoine Pécoud maps five competing philosophies of migration governance (rights-based, development, security, migration management, and cosmopolitan). Provides a rigorous conceptual framework for evaluating existing and future global migration governance.

YouTube Migration Forum | International Migration Review Forum at the UN
SABC News · 2022 · 5:04

A news report on the International Migration Review Forum at the UN, capturing the multilateral diplomacy and political tensions in implementing the GCM. Useful for grounding abstract governance concepts in current international negotiations and the politicization of migration.
