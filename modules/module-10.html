10 Module overview

Explores how migration shapes cities, local institutions, and community responses, including housing, education, services, community-led and refugee-led solutions, and the tension between integration and exclusion.

Skill emphasis: Applied + Systems

◎ Learning outcomes

Undergraduate

  1. Explain migration impacts on cities and integration challenges
  2. Identify support systems and integration services
  3. Describe the role of communities and community-led solutions
  4. Analyze community responses to migration

Graduate

  1. Evaluate urban integration policies and models
  2. Design community-based interventions
  3. Analyze the role of local governance in migrant integration

▤ Peer-reviewed readings

Show 5 readings5
  • DOISanctuary City as Mobilising Metaphor: How Sanctuary Articulates Urban Governance
    Rachel Humphris · 2023 · Journal of Ethnic and Migration Studies, 49(13), 3401–3417 · 10.1080/1369183X.2023.2198808

    Long-term ethnographic study of Sheffield as the UK's first self-designated 'City of Sanctuary,' revealing the contested and sometimes co-opted politics of local welcome. Essential for teaching how sanctuary city discourse navigates the boundary between symbolic inclusion and structural transformation.

    Access article →
  • DOIContesting Sanctuary: Urban Citizenship and Multilevel Migration Governance in Barcelona
    C. Delclós, Olatz Ribera-Almandoz, B. Garcés-Mascareñas · 2025 · Urban Studies, advance online · 10.1177/00420980251387828

    Multi-method study of Barcelona's 'City of Refuge' programme, showing how municipal actors mobilised to include undocumented residents while navigating hierarchical national and EU migration governance. Directly addresses local governance, sanctuary policies, and the limitations of municipal action.

    Access article →
  • DOIWhat Drives Local Action? Proactive Reception and Early Integration for Ukrainian Forced Migrants in Polish Cities: A Qualitative Comparative Analysis
    Igor Lyubashenko, D. Wojtowicz · 2026 · Urban Affairs Review, advance online · 10.1177/10780874261439763

    Identifies configurations of political leadership, institutional capacity, and community attitudes that drive proactive municipal reception of forced migrants in a centralised governance context. Provides a comparative model for teaching local integration governance.

    Access article →
  • DOINow You See Them, Now You Don't: Performance and the Politics of Localizing (Forced) Migration Governance in the Horn of Africa's Secondary Cities
    C. Kihato, L. Landau · 2024 · Urban Studies, 62(3), 589–606 · 10.1177/09562478241276707

    Analyses a Cities Alliance initiative across Somaliland, Kenya, Uganda, and Ethiopia that reduced host–refugee tensions through 'frontstage' visibility to funders and 'backstage' integration into urban planning. Ideal for teaching refugee-led and community-led solutions in non-Western urban contexts.

    Access article →
  • DOI'Living Their Best Life': PhotoVoice Insights on Well-Being, Inclusion, and Access to Public Spaces Among Adolescent Refugee Girls in Urban Resettlement
    Alli Gillespie, Carine Allaf, Ilana Seff, Lily Coll, Lindsay Stark, Manar Matti, Zahyyeh Abu-Rubieh · 2024 · International Journal of Qualitative Methods, 23 · 10.1080/17482631.2024.2431183

    Participatory photovoice research with adolescent refugee girls, centring their voices on well-being, belonging, and access to public urban spaces. Demonstrates community-led methodologies and highlights the educational role of schools in facilitating urban inclusion.

    Access article →

▷ Video resources

Show 5 videos5
YouTube Germany Opened Its Doors to Millions of Refugees — Here's How It Worked Out | DW News
DW News · 2025 · 24:56

Ten years after Germany's 2015 open-door policy, DW News examines integration outcomes, economic contributions, political backlash, and community-level responses. A rich case study for discussing how cities and communities absorb large refugee populations over time.

YouTube A Safe Pathway to Resettlement for Migrants and Refugees | Becca Heller | TED
TED · 2022 · 11:14

Human rights lawyer Becca Heller (IRAP) argues that legal resettlement pathways can be made accessible to millions more refugees with political will and innovation. Faculty can use this to discuss structural solutions to urban refugee settlement and community-based advocacy.

YouTube Integration and Contribution of Immigrants | Waheed Arian | TEDxLeicesterSalon
TEDx Talks · 2018 · 13:07

Afghan-born physician Waheed Arian speaks from personal experience as a refugee and NHS doctor about how integration serves both host communities and migrants. A powerful first-person narrative suitable for discussion of integration policies and community belonging.

YouTube Let's Help Refugees Thrive, Not Just Survive | Melissa Fleming | TED
TED · 2014 · 16:13

Former UNHCR spokesperson Melissa Fleming (now UN Under-Secretary-General) makes the case for transforming refugee camps into communities of opportunity rather than warehouses of despair. Useful for discussing refugee-led solutions and community-centered integration approaches.

YouTube Churches as Safe Havens: How Communities Protect Refugees from Deportation | DW Documentary
DW Documentary · 2023 · 27:26

DW documents how faith communities in Germany have transformed into sanctuary spaces protecting refugees from deportation, raising questions about civic solidarity and sanctuary city principles. Bridges Module 10 (sanctuary communities) with Module 11 (religion and hospitality).
