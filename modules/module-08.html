8 Module overview

Explores how migration interacts with global labor markets, remittances, and economic development, including migrant labor exploitation, livelihoods, entrepreneurship, financial inclusion, and informal economies.

Skill emphasis: Applied + Economic

◎ Learning outcomes

Undergraduate

  1. Explain migrant labor contributions to economies
  2. Describe remittances and their impacts
  3. Identify sectors relying on migrant labor and recognize exploitation risks
  4. Discuss challenges faced by migrant workers

Graduate

  1. Analyze global labor migration systems
  2. Design economic inclusion strategies
  3. Evaluate debates on migration, labor rights, and development

▤ Peer-reviewed readings

Show 6 readings6
  • DOIMigration and Development: A Theoretical Perspective
    Hein de Haas · 2010 · International Migration Review, 44(1), 227–264 · 10.1111/j.1747-7379.2009.00804.x

    A foundational review article that critically surveys optimistic and pessimistic theories of migration-development linkages, proposing an integrative 'aspiration-capability' framework. Essential for faculty teaching the contested relationship between remittances, migration, and development outcomes in origin countries.

    Access article →
  • DOITheories of International Migration: A Review and Appraisal
    Douglas S. Massey, J. Arango, Graeme Hugo, A. Kouaouci, Adela Pellegrino, J. E. Taylor · 1993 · Population and Development Review, 19(3), 431–466 · 10.2307/2938462

    The canonical comparative review of migration theories—neoclassical economics, new economics, dual labor market, and world systems theory. Still the standard entry point for teaching the structural economic drivers of migration and its global labor market dimensions.

    Access article →
  • DOISituating Platform Gig Economy in the Formal Subsumption of Reproductive Labor: Transnational Migrant Domestic Workers and the Continuum of Exploitation and Precarity
    Siyuan Yin · 2023 · Capital & Class, 47(1), 35–55 · 10.1177/03098168221145407

    Examines how digital labor platforms extend and intensify exploitation of transnational migrant domestic workers, situating platform capitalism within histories of racialised and gendered labor control. Directly addresses migrant labor exploitation and the intersection of immigration policy with informal economies.

    Access article →
  • DOILabor Migration, Remittances, and the Economy in the Gulf Cooperation Council Region
    Udaya R. Wagle · 2024 · Comparative Migration Studies, 12(1), article 29 · 10.1186/s40878-024-00390-3

    Provides recent empirical analysis of the complex and sometimes negative macro-level relationship between mass labor migration and economic growth in the Gulf, a major destination region. Useful for complicating simplistic narratives about remittances as unambiguous development tools.

    Access article →
  • DOIPlatform Work in the Domestic and Home Care Sector: New Mechanisms of Invisibility and Exploitation of Women Migrant Workers
    Paula Rodríguez-Modroño, Astrid Agenjo-Calderón, Purificación López-Igual · 2022 · Work, Employment and Society, 37(4), 983–999 · 10.1080/13552074.2022.2121060

    Applies feminist political economy to show how care platforms exploit intersecting inequalities of gender, race, and immigration status among migrant women workers in the Global South. Connects migrant labor exploitation to care chains, precarious livelihoods, and platform capitalism.

    Access article →
  • DOIDigital Financial Inclusion and Remittances: An Empirical Study on Bangladeshi Migrant Households
    Kazi Abdul Mannan, Khandaker Mursheda Farhana · 2023 · FinTech, 2(4), 556–570 · 10.3390/fintech2040038

    Empirical study demonstrating how migrant remittances drive adoption of digital financial services in Bangladesh, providing evidence on financial inclusion mechanisms. Relevant for teaching financial inclusion, remittance flows, and the economic impact on origin-country households.

    Access article →

▷ Video resources

Show 5 videos5
YouTube The Hidden Force in Global Economics: Sending Money Home
TED / Dilip Ratha (World Bank) · 2014 · 17:04

World Bank economist Dilip Ratha explains how the $413 billion migrants sent home in 2013 dwarfs global foreign aid, making remittances a critical lever for development. Faculty can use this to open discussions on the macroeconomic role of migrant labor and the hidden architecture of global financial flows.

YouTube The Economics of Remittances: Is Migrant Work Worth It? | In Conversation | Dilip Ratha, World Bank
CNA Insider · 2022 · 22:29

An in-depth interview with Dilip Ratha (World Bank) that examines whether migrant work is economically rational for workers and sending countries, covering remittance fees, pandemic impacts, and development trade-offs. Useful for case-study analysis of the costs and benefits of labor migration.

YouTube The Brutal Economics of Migrant Labour
Economics Explained · 2022 · 13:12

A data-driven explainer covering why migrants move for work, the global labor shortage, remittance flows, and negative impacts on both sending and receiving economies. Good for introducing students to the structural economic forces driving labor migration.

YouTube Human Trafficking Is All Around You. This Is How It Works
TED / Noy Thrupkaew · 2015 · 18:52

Journalist Noy Thrupkaew exposes how forced labor and human trafficking are embedded in global supply chains that produce everyday goods. Faculty can use this TED talk to shift discussions from remittances to the darker side of migrant labor, including exploitation and trafficking.

YouTube How Migrant Workers Are Vital to the U.S. Economy
CBS News · 2023 · 4:57

A concise news segment illustrating U.S. dependence on migrant workers across agriculture, construction, and services, set against recent tragedies involving migrant workers. Useful as a brief current-events entry point before deeper discussion of labor rights and exploitation.
