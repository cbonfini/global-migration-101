4 Module overview

Examines historical migration patterns including colonial migration, slavery, industrial migration, and post-war displacement, and how religious and cultural traditions framed the idea of welcoming the stranger. Connects historical patterns to contemporary migration.

Skill emphasis: Conceptual + Comparative

◎ Learning outcomes

Undergraduate

  1. Identify key historical migration events and religious framings of welcoming the stranger
  2. Explain how historical migration and hospitality narratives shaped societies
  3. Describe connections between colonialism and migration systems
  4. Connect historical patterns to contemporary migration

Graduate

  1. Analyze how historical migration systems and cultural/religious narratives shape present debates and policy
  2. Analyze continuity and rupture in migration regimes
  3. Critically assess historical migration narratives

▤ Peer-reviewed readings

Show 6 readings6
  • DOIMigration History and Historiography
    Rossi, Benedetta · 2018 · Oxford Research Encyclopedia of African History. Oxford University Press · 10.1093/acrefore/9780190277734.013.325

    A comprehensive survey of African migration history from deep pre-history through the transatlantic slave trade, colonial forced labour, and contemporary migration patterns. Essential for a Global South perspective on historical migration and for grounding discussions of colonialism's role in shaping current migration systems.

    Access article →
  • DOIThe Age of Intra-African Migration: Shifting Patterns of Regional Mobility Between Two Global Diasporas, 1850–1960
    Frankema, Ewout & de Haas, Michiel Bart · 2025 · Comparative Migration Studies, 13(1), 19 · 10.1186/s40878-025-00448-w

    Analyses the understudied era of intra-African regional mobility between 1850 and 1960, challenging the notion that contemporary African mobility is unprecedented. Directly relevant to debunking myths about African migration and situating current flows in a longer historical arc of slavery, colonial labour, and post-independence patterns.

    Access article →
  • DOIGlobal Spatio-Temporal Patterns in Human Migration: A Complex Network Perspective
    Laio, Francesco; Davis, Kyle K.; Ridolfi, Luca; & D'Odorico, Paolo · 2013 · PLOS ONE, 8(1), e53723 · 10.1371/journal.pone.0053723

    Examines how the global migration network became more interconnected from 1960 to 2000, and shows that destination choices reflect colonial histories, language, and religious ties. This data-driven piece vividly illustrates how colonial legacies continue to structure modern migration systems.

    Access article →
  • DOIRefugee Policy Amidst Global Shocks: Encampment, Resettlement Barriers and the Search for 'Durable Solutions'
    Spiegel, Paul B. & Mhlanga, Jabulani · 2022 · Global Policy, 13(3), 386–399 · 10.1111/1758-5899.13084

    Traces the evolution of global refugee policy from the 1951 Convention through COVID-era restrictions, using Zimbabwe's Tongogara camp as a case study. Contrasts colonial policy orientations with African Ubuntu philosophies, making it ideal for teaching religious and cultural narratives of hospitality alongside policy evolution.

    Access article →
  • DOIDurable Displacement and the Protracted Search for Solutions: Promising Programs and Strategies
    Ferris, Elizabeth & Kerwin, Donald M. · 2023 · Journal on Migration and Human Security, 11(1), 1–25 · 10.1177/23315024231160454

    Introduces a special issue covering 10 cases of protracted displacement across three continents, synthesizing what durable solutions have worked and why they remain elusive. Historically situates post-WWII displacement solutions within contemporary crises, illustrating continuity in the challenges facing displaced populations.

    Access article →
  • DOIA European Narrative of Border Externalisation: The European Trust Fund for Africa Story
    Pacciardi, Agnese · 2024 · European Security, 33(1), 91–110 · 10.1080/09662839.2024.2304723

    Traces the historical development of EU border externalization policy through the EU Trust Fund for Africa, showing how migration containment strategies evolved from post-WWII cooperation frameworks. Bridges Module 4's historical policy evolution with Module 7's contemporary borders and sovereignty discussions.

    Access article →

YouTube Migration: A Historical Perspective - Professor Sir Richard Evans
Gresham College · 2016 · 55:17

Regius Professor Sir Richard Evans delivers a sweeping Gresham College public lecture tracing migration from antiquity through colonialism to contemporary movements, situating current flows within deep historical context. An authoritative, lecture-format resource for grounding Week 4 in historical analysis.

YouTube Human Traffic: Race and Post-War Migration Policy
Gresham College (Royal Historical Society Colin Matthew Memorial Lecture) · 2019 · 48:07

The 2019 Royal Historical Society memorial lecture examines how race and empire shaped post-WWII migration policies in Britain and Europe. Directly addresses colonial legacies in migration governance, critical for Week 4's intersection of history and policy.

YouTube The Great Migration: Crash Course Black American History #24
CrashCourse · 2021 · 12:40

CrashCourse provides an accessible, well-sourced account of the Great Migration of Black Americans from the South (1910–1970), covering causes, scale, and societal impact. A clear case study in domestic historical migration that illustrates push/pull dynamics at scale.

YouTube CAUSES of Migration 1750–1900 [AP World History] Unit 6 Topic 6
Heimler's History · 2022 · 7:21

Covers the major historical migration waves of the Industrial era including abolition of slavery, indentured labor systems, and colonially driven movements. Provides structured historical framing for the long 19th century period often neglected in migration courses.

YouTube 'Refugee World(s): a Twentieth-Century Retrospective' – the 2025 Prothero Lecture with Peter Gatrell
Royal Historical Society · 2025 · 52:08

Leading refugee historian Professor Peter Gatrell (University of Manchester) delivers the Royal Historical Society's 2025 Prothero Lecture, offering a panoramic retrospective on refugee history across the 20th century. An authoritative scholarly lecture for contextualizing the modern refugee regime.
