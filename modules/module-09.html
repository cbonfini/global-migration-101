9 Module overview

Examines migrant identity, belonging, cultural adaptation, and transnational communities, attending to cross-cutting identities such as age, gender, sexual orientation, disability, statelessness, intersectionality, racism, and exclusion.

Skill emphasis: Analytical + Reflective

◎ Learning outcomes

Undergraduate

  1. Describe migrant adaptation challenges and identity dynamics
  2. Explain identity, belonging, and intersectionality in migration contexts
  3. Identify factors influencing integration and forms of exclusion
  4. Analyze migrant narratives and recognize diversity

Graduate

  1. Analyze migration’s impact on identity and transnational networks
  2. Apply intersectional analysis
  3. Evaluate integration, multiculturalism, and identity-based policy impacts

▤ Peer-reviewed readings

Show 6 readings6
  • DOIMethodological Nationalism and the Study of Migration
    Andreas Wimmer, Nina Glick Schiller · 2002 · European Journal of Sociology / Archives Européennes de Sociologie, 43(2), 217–240 · 10.1017/S000397560200108X

    A foundational critique exposing how 'methodological nationalism'—treating the nation-state as the natural unit of analysis—has distorted migration theory and obscured transnational social fields. Indispensable for framing why scholars must move beyond assimilationist integration frameworks.

    Access article →
  • DOIMethodological Nationalism and Beyond: Nation–State Building, Migration and the Social Sciences
    Andreas Wimmer, Nina Glick Schiller · 2002 · Global Networks, 2(4), 301–334 · 10.1111/1471-0374.00043

    Companion to the EJS piece, this article provides a longer historical account of how nation-state ideologies shaped immigration and integration policies and the social sciences. Provides the conceptual foundation for teaching transnationalism and the limits of assimilation models.

    Access article →
  • DOIRefugees, Migrants, Neither, Both: Categorical Fetishism and the Politics of Bounding in Europe's 'Migration Crisis'
    Heaven Crawley, Dimitris Skleparis · 2018 · Journal of Ethnic and Migration Studies, 44(1), 48–64 · 10.1080/1369183X.2017.1348224

    Challenges the sharp categorical distinction between 'refugees' and 'migrants' based on interviews with 215 Mediterranean crossers, showing these categories fail to capture lived identities and serve exclusionary political ends. Essential for discussions of statelessness, labelling, and the politics of identity.

    Access article →
  • DOIAn Intersectional Analysis of Gendered Precarity in Migration and Statelessness
    Cassadee Orinthia Yan · 2025 · International Conference on Social Science and Humanities (ICSSH) Proceedings, 5, 353–536 · 10.20319/icssh.2025.535536

    Examines how patriarchal nationality laws, migration controls, and economic subordination intersect to produce statelessness and exclusion for women and non-binary migrants. Directly addresses intersectionality, gender, statelessness, and the compounding of legal and economic exclusion.

    Access article →
  • DOIThe Intersections between Migration and Disability: Narratives by EU Migrants to the UK, Disabled British People and Disabled EU Migrants
    Eva A. Duda-Mikulin · 2024 · Social Sciences, 13(9), 493 · 10.3390/socsci13090493

    Qualitative study exploring the layered disadvantages faced when migration and disability intersect, revealing how barriers multiply and produce compounded social marginalisation. Fills a significant gap in migration studies on disability and intersectionality.

    Access article →
  • DOIIdentity Is a Matter of Place: Intersectional Identities of Romanian Women Migrant Entrepreneurs on the Eastern–Western European Route
    I. Chițac · 2023 · International Journal of Entrepreneurial Behavior & Research, 31(1), 80–108 · 10.1108/ijebr-10-2022-0897

    Uses Interpretative Phenomenological Analysis and Crenshaw's intersectionality framework to show how Romanian women entrepreneurs negotiate shifting gender, national, and entrepreneurial identities transnationally. Useful for teaching intersectionality, gender, and transnational belonging.

    Access article →

▷ Video resources

Show 5 videos5
YouTube A Migrant Crisis or a Crisis of Belonging? | Jess Thompson | TEDxFrensham
TEDx Talks · 2022 · 17:54

Jess Thompson (founder of Migrateful, Forbes 30 Under 30) draws on frontline experience in Dunkirk and Morocco to argue that the migrant 'crisis' is fundamentally a crisis of belonging and contact, not security. Faculty can use this to frame intersectionality and the social-psychological dimensions of migrant identity.

YouTube Cate Blanchett: What Is Statelessness? | #iBelong
UNHCR, the UN Refugee Agency · 2020 · 3:39

UNHCR Goodwill Ambassador Cate Blanchett explains the causes, consequences, and scale of statelessness (10+ million people) as part of UNHCR's #iBelong campaign. Useful as a brief, accessible explainer to introduce students to legal identity, statelessness, and the right to belong.

YouTube Statelessness: A Forgotten Human Rights Crisis
Pulitzer Center · 2019 · 5:55

A documentary short from the Pulitzer Center examining the lived realities of stateless people who lack citizenship in any country and face systematic exclusion from rights. Pairs well with the UNHCR statelessness resources to deepen student understanding of legal identity and vulnerability.

YouTube A Tale of Two Migrations: Chasing the American Dream | Al Jazeera Correspondent
Al Jazeera English · 2014 · 47:52

Through the story of one immigrant family, this documentary explores how race, assimilation, and identity evolve across generations of migrants in the United States. Faculty can use this long-form film to anchor discussions about second-generation identity, belonging, and the persistence of racism.

YouTube American Stories About Immigration and Belonging
New York Times Events · 2018 · 33:32

Five short documentary films presented by the New York Times that tell poignant personal stories of separation, identity, and belonging across immigrant communities. Suitable for facilitating small-group discussion about the emotional and cultural dimensions of migration.
