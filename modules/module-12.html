12 Module overview

Examines how migration is represented in media and public discourse, including framing, stereotypes, political narratives, misinformation, and the link between media and policy outcomes.

Skill emphasis: Analytical + Critical Thinking

◎ Learning outcomes

Undergraduate

  1. Identify common migration narratives and media bias
  2. Explain media influence on public perception
  3. Analyze migration coverage examples and compare sources
  4. Recognize misinformation and stereotypes

Graduate

  1. Critically analyze media framing of migration
  2. Critique narrative construction
  3. Evaluate the impact of narratives on policy and public opinion

▤ Peer-reviewed readings

Show 6 readings6
  • DOIRefugees, Migrants, Neither, Both: Categorical Fetishism and the Politics of Bounding in Europe's 'Migration Crisis'
    Heaven Crawley, Dimitris Skleparis · 2018 · Journal of Ethnic and Migration Studies, 44(1), 48–64 · 10.1080/1369183X.2017.1348224

    Demonstrates how the political deployment of 'refugee' vs. 'migrant' categories in European media and policy functioned to justify exclusion, grounded in interviews with Mediterranean crossers. Directly relevant for teaching how political narratives and categorical framing shape public perception.

    Access article →
  • DOIRefugees in the Media: Exploring a Vicious Cycle of Frustrated Psychological Needs, Selective Exposure, and Hostile Intergroup Attitudes
    Adrian Lueders, Eva Jonas, Michael Prentice · 2019 · European Journal of Social Psychology, 50(2), 316–330 · 10.1002/ejsp.2580

    Laboratory experiment showing how frustrated psychological needs shape selective exposure to negative refugee media coverage, reinforcing hostile attitudes and undermining integration. A compelling empirical piece for teaching the psychological mechanisms behind media effects on immigration opinion.

    Access article →
  • DOINegative Media Portrayals of Immigrants Increase Ingroup Favoritism and Hostile Physiological and Emotional Reactions
    C. Mosso, Federica Spaccatini, G. Fuochi, L. Anfossi, Pierluigi Conzo · 2021 · Scientific Reports, 11, 16622 · 10.1038/s41598-021-95800-2

    Experimental study demonstrating that exposure to negative immigration rhetoric in mass media measurably increases ingroup favoritism and physiological hostility toward immigrants. Useful for grounding media effects research in psychophysiology and the real-world consequences of dehumanizing discourse.

    Access article →
  • DOIHow Security Framing Creates Disagreement in Public Opinion: Evidence from US Refugee Policy
    I. Skinner · 2025 · Political Research Quarterly, 78(2), 556–570 · 10.1080/21565503.2025.2466550

    Survey experiment showing that securitization rhetoric polarises public attitudes on refugee admissions along partisan lines, while humanitarian frames have more cross-partisan appeal. Directly addresses how political communication and framing interact with public opinion on immigration.

    Access article →
  • DOIVisual (De)humanization: Construction of Otherness in Newspaper Photographs of the Refugee Crisis
    Inari Sakki, Jari Martikainen · 2021 · Ethnic and Racial Studies, 44(14), 2572–2592 · 10.1080/01419870.2021.1965178

    Content analysis of 1,473 newspaper images identifies six visual rhetorical strategies—including massifying, demonizing, and passivating—that dehumanize refugees. Valuable for teaching visual discourse analysis and the role of photographs in constructing stereotypes and 'threat' narratives.

    Access article →
  • DOIDeserving of Assistance: The Social Construction of Ukrainian Refugees
    Agnieszka Zogata-Kusz, Milena Öbrink Hobzová, Rafał Cekiera · 2023 · Journal of Refugee Studies, 37(2), 368–383 · 10.1080/17449057.2023.2286779

    Qualitative content analysis of Polish and Czech media showing how Ukrainian refugees were constructed as 'deserving' through cultural proximity and White European identity—illuminating racialized hierarchies in refugee narratives and differential media framing. Essential for teaching misinformation, selectivity, and racial bias in media.

    Access article →

▷ Video resources

Show 4 videos4
YouTube Between 'Swarms' and 'Security': How Media Report on Migration | The Listening Post (Full)
Al Jazeera English · 2019 · 26:25

Al Jazeera's media-analysis program The Listening Post features correspondents from The Intercept, Vox, the Migration Policy Institute, and The Guardian dissecting how language ('swarms,' 'invasion') shapes public fear and policy. A high-quality journalism studies resource on framing and narrative power.

YouTube How Lies About Migration Polarize Societies | Anatomy of Disinformation
TVP World · 2026 · 25:25

A recent (2026) analysis of how disinformation about migration spreads online, how the 'click economy' monetizes fear, and how pre-election manipulation weaponizes migrant narratives. Excellent for discussing social media's role in shaping public opinion and the U.S. political paradox of a nation of immigrants.

YouTube Reporting Migration: The Role of Media in Shaping Public Opinion and Policies on Migration
ICMPD (International Centre for Migration Policy Development) · 2021 · 1:22:22

A practitioner-focused webinar from ICMPD introducing their Handbook on Reporting Migration, examining how media coverage constructs migration as a political object and offering strategies for responsible reporting. Suitable as a more policy-oriented complement to Al Jazeera's framing analysis.

YouTube Migrants Are Falling Prey to Social Media Misinformation | NBC News
NBC News · 2022 · 4:26

NBC News reports on how asylum seekers are misled by false claims on social media — such as fake reports that the U.S.-Mexico border is open — with real-world consequences for their journeys and safety. Illustrates how misinformation harms migrants themselves, not just public opinion.
