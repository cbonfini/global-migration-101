11 Module overview

Explores ethical frameworks shaping migration debates including religious traditions, Catholic social teaching, hospitality vs exclusion, and alternative lenses such as abolition and theology.

Skill emphasis: Reflective + Ethical Reasoning

◎ Learning outcomes

Undergraduate

  1. Describe ethical perspectives on migration
  2. Explain Catholic social teaching and religious influences related to migration
  3. Identify roles of faith communities
  4. Analyze moral dilemmas and ethical debates about migration policies

Graduate

  1. Critically evaluate ethical frameworks shaping migration policy
  2. Apply ethical frameworks to policy debates
  3. Analyze the role of religious actors in migration responses

▤ Peer-reviewed readings

Show 6 readings6
  • DOIReligious Engagement and the Migration Issue: Towards Reconciling Political and Moral Duty
    Joanna Kulska · 2020 · Religions, 11(5), 236 · 10.3390/rel11050236

    Argues that religious non-state actors must be recognised as 'trend-setters' on the ethical dimensions of migration, mediating between security-driven state policies and moral obligations to migrants. Strong overview for teaching the normative role of faith communities in migration governance.

    Access article →
  • DOIThe Trials and Consolations of Migrant-Serving Faith-Based Organizations
    Donald Kerwin · 2024 · Journal of Law and Religion, 39(2), 253–276 · 10.1017/jlr.2024.9

    Written from the perspective of US Christian faith-based organizations serving migrants, this article outlines the theological foundations of migrant hospitality and the challenges posed by political polarization. Directly relevant to Catholic social teaching, faith-based advocacy, and the ethics of hospitality vs. exclusion.

    Access article →
  • DOIResearching Religion and Migration 20 Years after '9/11': Taking Stock and Looking Ahead
    Fenella Fleischmann · 2022 · Zeitschrift für Religion, Gesellschaft und Politik, 6, 347–372 · 10.1007/s41682-022-00103-6

    Reviews two decades of survey research on immigrant religiosity in Europe, assessing whether religion bridges or barriers to integration—with inconclusive but illuminating findings. Excellent for teaching the complexity of the religion–migration–integration nexus and directions for future research.

    Access article →
  • DOICan Appeals to Religious Values Mitigate Anti-Refugee Sentiment? Experimental Evidence from Lebanon
    Sam Selsky · 2024 · Journal of International Migration and Integration, 25(3), 1447–1468 · 10.1177/10659129241263072

    Survey experiment testing whether invoking sacred texts and religious duty shifts attitudes toward refugee inclusion, finding meaningful effects for high-animosity sectarian groups. Directly connects religious ethics to empirical questions about hospitality, exclusion, and the borders of moral community.

    Access article →
  • DOIA Mid-Level Perspective on the Ethics of Immigration Policies
    Rainer Bauböck · 2025 · Critical Review of International Social and Political Philosophy, 28(3), 441–460 · 10.1080/13698230.2025.2489247

    Critically engages Carens's open-borders argument, proposing a 'mid-level' approach that promotes expanded international free movement through liberal democratic reform rather than ideal-theory advocacy. Offers students a rigorous point of debate in the open-borders vs. state-sovereignty ethics discussion.

    Access article →
  • DOIReligious and Non-Religious Forms of Hospitality: Muslim and Jewish Engagement in Welcoming Newcomers in Britain
    E. Braginskaia · 2023 · Journal of Church and State, 65(4), 439–459 · 10.1093/jcs/csad069

    Comparative study of how Muslim and Jewish communities in Britain interpret and practice hospitality toward migrants, examining the theological and secular grounds for welcome. Enriches cross-religious perspectives and the concept of faith-based hospitality.

    Access article →

▷ Video resources

Show 5 videos5
YouTube Open Borders? Immigration, Citizenship, and Nationalism in the 21st Century | Janus Forum Series
Brown University · 2018 · 1:50:03

A structured academic debate between Joseph Carens (University of Toronto, open borders advocate) and David Miller (Oxford, restrictionist) on the ethical foundations of immigration policy, national identity, and migrants' rights. Faculty can assign segments or screen key arguments to model academic philosophical debate.

YouTube Joseph Carens — The Ethics of Immigration
Science4Peace / University of Toronto · 2017 · 53:13

A lecture by Joseph Carens, the leading theorist of open borders, presenting his moral argument that freedom of movement is a fundamental human right. Essential for engaging with canonical ethics-of-migration literature alongside his book 'The Ethics of Immigration.'

YouTube "Ethics and the Refugee Crisis": Serena Parekh in Conversation with David Owen
The Philosopher · 2024 · 58:56

Philosophers Serena Parekh (Northeastern) and David Owen (Southampton) discuss who is responsible for refugees, what obligations states and citizens hold, and how to end the 'crisis of arrival.' Directly applicable to Catholic social teaching's concepts of solidarity, human dignity, and the right to asylum.

YouTube What the Catholic Church Really Teaches About Immigration
Good Catholic · 2021 · 6:26

A concise overview of Catholic social teaching on immigration, grounded in the Catechism's articulation of the rights of migrants and responsibilities of host nations. Ideal as a starter video before deeper engagement with Church documents like Laudato Si' or statements from USCCB.

YouTube Hospitality in a Culture of Displacement | Fred Liggin | TEDxFurmanU
TEDx Talks · 2017 · 13:57

Drawing on cross-cultural and religious traditions, this talk reframes hospitality as a moral and spiritual imperative in contexts of displacement. Useful for multi-faith discussions on the ethics of welcoming migrants and refugees from Abrahamic and other religious perspectives.
