5 Module overview

Focuses on migration routes, transit experiences, and the risks migrants encounter during journeys, including trafficking, exploitation, trauma, gendered and child-specific vulnerabilities, and resilience.

Skill emphasis: Reflective + Applied

◎ Learning outcomes

Undergraduate

  1. Identify major global migration routes and stages of the journey
  2. Describe challenges and risks during migration journeys
  3. Explain how geography and policy shape routes and emotional impacts
  4. Recognize vulnerable groups and analyze case studies of journeys

Graduate

  1. Evaluate geopolitical factors and border regimes shaping routes
  2. Evaluate trauma-informed frameworks
  3. Apply ethical engagement principles to lived-experience material

▤ Peer-reviewed readings

Show 6 readings6
  • DOITorture, Trauma and Posttraumatic Symptoms in Syrian Women Asylum Seekers in the Greek Border Camp of Idomeni
    Guarch-Rubio, Marta; Manzanero, Antonio L.; Roy Delgado, Francisco; Minescu, Anca; & Mohamed, D. · 2025 · Torture Journal, 35(1), 1–17 · 10.7146/torture.v35i1.140862

    Documents high rates of PTSD (78–91%) among Syrian and Kurdish women blocked at the Idomeni camp, linking trauma to both pre-migration violence and conditions in transit camps. Provides concrete evidence for the gendered and cumulative nature of journey-related trauma and the concept of 'torturing environments'.

    Access article →
  • DOI'I Feel Like I Am Less Than Other People': Health-Related Vulnerabilities of Male Migrants Travelling Alone on Their Journey to Europe
    Arsenijević, Jelena; Burtscher, Dejan; Ponthieu, Annick; et al. · 2018 · Social Science & Medicine, 209, 86–94 · 10.1016/j.socscimed.2018.05.038

    Qualitative study with male migrants along the Balkan route reveals how deterrence measures (pushbacks, detention) amplify psychological distress and create 'traditionally invisible' vulnerability. Challenges gender assumptions in humanitarian response and demonstrates how border enforcement policies deepen journey-related harm.

    Access article →
  • DOIBeyond the Border: Clandestine Migration Journeys
    Mainwaring, Ċetta & Brigden, Noelle K. · 2016 · Geopolitics, 21(2), 243–262 · 10.1080/14650045.2016.1165575

    Conceptualizes clandestine migration journeys as a series of cumulatively dangerous passages shaped by geography, state enforcement, and smuggling networks. A key theoretical framework for teaching routes, transit, and the spatial dimensions of irregularized migration that bridge smuggling, risk, and resilience.

    Access article →
  • DOIBad Deals and Tragic Pacts: The Impact of the EU's Management of Migration Through the Externalisation of its Borders on 'Women on the Move'
    Vella, Mary Grace · 2024 · Open Research Europe, 4(188), 1 · 10.12688/openreseurope.18388.1

    Critically examines how the EU–Turkey Deal and subsequent agreements expose women and girls to heightened risks through securitized border management. Directly addresses gender-specific vulnerabilities in migration journeys, border externalization, and the protection gaps created by containment-focused policies.

    Access article →
  • DOIFrom Forced Migration to Forced Arrival: The Campization of Refugee Accommodation in European Cities
    Kreichauf, René · 2018 · Comparative Migration Studies, 6(1), 7 · 10.1186/s40878-017-0069-8

    Introduces the concept of 'campization'—the extension of camp logics into urban arrival spaces—analyzing how containment, exclusion, and temporality are reproduced in cities. Bridges the journey–destination gap and is essential for teaching how route-end conditions continue to shape migrant experiences and resilience.

    Access article →
  • DOIMigrant Deaths in California's Borderlands, 2018–2023
    LaFleur, Marni; On, Kevin; Ceja, Ligia; & Martínez, Daniel E. · 2024 · Journal on Migration and Human Security, 12(4), 234–259 · 10.1177/23315024241291825

    Documents 314 migrant deaths in southern California over five years, linking spikes in mortality to border wall construction and COVID-era deterrence policies. Provides stark empirical data on the lethal consequences of border enforcement, making it highly impactful for teaching about route risks and the ethics of deterrence.

    Access article →

▷ Video resources

Show 5 videos5
YouTube Europe's deadly migration policy | DW Documentary
DW Documentary · 2023 · 51:55

DW's investigative documentary examines how EU migration policy outsources border enforcement to North Africa, funneling migrants onto deadlier routes and enabling exploitation by smugglers. Provides systemic analysis of how policy shapes journey risk — essential for Week 5.

YouTube Migrating to the US through the deadly Darien Gap | Fault Lines Documentary
Al Jazeera English · 2022 · 25:01

Al Jazeera's Fault Lines follows migrants crossing the Darien Gap — one of the world's most deadly migration routes — providing ground-level insight into the dangers, motivations, and demographics of the US-bound migration corridor. Ideal for humanizing the abstract concept of 'migration routes.'

YouTube CBS Reports | Darien Gap: Desperate Journey to America
CBS News · 2023 · 27:37

CBS News reports on the Darien Gap as thousands cross annually through 60 miles of dangerous jungle, providing firsthand documentary coverage of this key Americas migration corridor. Complements the Al Jazeera Fault Lines piece with an American network perspective.

YouTube This is the world's deadliest border | Vox
Vox · 2015 · 3:40

Vox's explainer on migrant deaths in the Mediterranean documents the scale of mortality on Europe-bound sea routes and contextualizes European border enforcement. A concise, data-driven piece appropriate for introducing the concept of border lethality.

YouTube Separated: Children at the Border (full documentary) | FRONTLINE PBS
FRONTLINE PBS · 2018 · 53:15

FRONTLINE's award-winning documentary investigates the US family separation policy at the US–Mexico border, tracing migration journeys from Central America and exposing the human cost of enforcement strategies. Provides a powerful case study linking migration journeys, policy, and human rights.
