13 Module overview

Explores how environmental change and climate impacts influence migration patterns, including slow- vs rapid-onset events, legal gaps, environmental justice, and interaction with economic migration.

Skill emphasis: Systems Thinking

◎ Learning outcomes

Undergraduate

  1. Explain environmental drivers of migration
  2. Identify regions vulnerable to climate displacement
  3. Describe climate migration concepts and legal gaps
  4. Recognize the global impact and policy challenges

Graduate

  1. Evaluate policy frameworks addressing climate migration
  2. Model future climate migration scenarios
  3. Analyze links between climate vulnerability and displacement

▤ Peer-reviewed readings

Show 6 readings6
  • DOILessons Learned and Policy Implications from Climate-Related Planned Relocation in Fiji and Australia
    Annah E. Piggott-McKellar, Karen J. Vella · 2023 · Frontiers in Climate, 5, article 1032547 · 10.3389/fclim.2023.1032547

    Draws on empirical case studies of planned relocation in Fiji and Australia to extract lessons and policy recommendations for communities displaced by slow-onset and rapid-onset climate events. Bridges climate science, environmental justice, and community-led adaptation, filling the gap between policy and practice.

    Access article →
  • DOIClimate Change, Migration, and Displacement: Advancing a Risk-Informed Approach for Sustainable Solutions
    S. Mugambiwa, Patience Sibanda · 2025 · International Journal of Politics and Security, 11(4) · 10.36922/ijps.5165

    Reviews intersections of climate change and migration/displacement in global literature, arguing for risk management and climate justice frameworks embedded in sustainable development. Provides a strong interdisciplinary synthesis of the policy landscape for climate displacement.

    Access article →
  • DOIClimate Displacement and Legal Gaps: Advancing Human Rights and Digital Environmental Governance in Indonesia
    Mohammad Hazyar Arumbinang · 2025 · E3S Web of Conferences, 660, 02004 · 10.1051/e3sconf/202566002004

    Identifies critical legal deficiencies in protecting climate-displaced communities in Indonesia and examines how digital governance tools (civil registries, early warning systems) can bridge legal gaps. Useful for teaching environmental justice and the intersection of law, governance, and technological adaptation.

    Access article →
  • DOIEnvironmentally Induced Human Displacement and Human Rights: Bridging Legal Gaps Through a Human Rights-Based Approach
    Dimitris Chotouras, Roxani Fragkou, A. Tsadiras · 2024 · Human Rights Law Review, 13(2), 412–435 · 10.1163/22131035-13020001

    Advocates for a human rights-based approach to environmentally induced displacement, arguing that recognising a right to a healthy environment in a binding instrument would bridge existing legal protection gaps. Directly addresses legal frameworks and the gap in international refugee/migration law for climate displaced persons.

    Access article →
  • DOIHow Well Can We Predict Climate Migration? A Review of Forecasting Models
    Andrea Milan, B. Madson, Carol Farbotko, K. Schewel et al. · 2024 · Frontiers in Climate, 5, article 1189125 · 10.3389/fclim.2023.1189125

    Reviews 30 recent climate migration forecasting models, assessing their underlying assumptions, data sources, and projective uncertainty. Essential for teaching students how to critically evaluate climate displacement projections—from World Bank Groundswell figures to micro-level household models.

    Access article →
  • DOIAttributing Climate-Change-Related Disaster Displacement Responsibilities Along Global Production Chains
    Guadalupe Arce, J. Zafrilla, Mateo Ortiz, Á. García-Alaminos · 2024 · iScience, 27(11), 111124 · 10.1016/j.isci.2024.111124

    Quantifies responsibility for climate-induced displacement by linking consumption-based emissions in wealthy nations to displacement vulnerability in low-income countries via global supply chains. A novel environmental justice framework connecting historical consumption to climate displacement projections.

    Access article →

▷ Video resources

Show 5 videos5
YouTube Climate Change Will Displace Millions. Here's How We Prepare | Colette Pichon Battle | TED
TED · 2020 · 12:48

Climate justice lawyer Colette Pichon Battle draws on her community's displacement after Hurricane Katrina to argue that climate migration requires a fundamental restructuring of economic and legal systems. One of the most cited TED talks on climate displacement — ideal as a core reading for this module.

YouTube Climate Change Isn't a Distant Threat — It's Our Reality | Selina Neirok Leem | TED
TED · 2015 · 6:11

A young Marshall Islander speaks about rising seas, flooding, and the imminent erasure of her homeland, giving a first-person voice to Pacific small island communities facing complete displacement. Pairs powerfully with data on sea-level rise to personalize the existential stakes of climate migration.

YouTube Climate-Change Migrants: What Can Be Done?
The Economist · 2023 · 10:27

The Economist examines projections for climate-driven migration, what countries can do to prepare for mass displacement, and the policy gaps in current international frameworks. Useful for discussing future scenarios and governance challenges around climate refugees.

YouTube Does Climate Change Cause Migration? | Daniela Paredes Grijalva | TEDxDonauinsel
TEDx Talks · 2022 · 13:36

Researcher Daniela Paredes Grijalva complicates simple climate-migration causality, arguing that intersecting factors (livelihoods, social capital, colonial legacies) mediate who can and cannot move. A nuanced academic counterpoint that helps students think critically about climate migration narratives.

YouTube Climate Exodus: The Real Stories Behind Climate Migration | Seed Documentary
Seed · 2023 · 54:05

A feature-length documentary following individuals displaced by climate change — including flooding in Bangladesh — to illustrate the human dimension of environmental displacement. Suitable for a documentary screening paired with discussion of legal frameworks for 'climate refugees.'
