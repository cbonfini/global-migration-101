Global Migration 101 · Historical & Structural Context

Migration Through History

Faculty teaching handout · Skill emphasis: Conceptual + Comparative

Examines historical migration patterns including colonial migration, slavery, industrial migration, and post-war displacement, and how religious and cultural traditions framed the idea of welcoming the stranger. Connects historical patterns to contemporary migration.

Undergraduate outcomes

  1. Identify key historical migration events and religious framings of welcoming the stranger
  2. Explain how historical migration and hospitality narratives shaped societies
  3. Describe connections between colonialism and migration systems
  4. Connect historical patterns to contemporary migration

Graduate outcomes

  1. Analyze how historical migration systems and cultural/religious narratives shape present debates and policy
  2. Analyze continuity and rupture in migration regimes
  3. Critically assess historical migration narratives

Key topics & scope

Major migration wavescolonialism and slaveryindustrial migrationpost-war displacementpolicy evolutionreligious narratives of hospitalityhistorical parallels to today

Signature learning experience

Comparative essay: past vs present migration system
